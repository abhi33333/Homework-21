Web Redesign Case Study

UX/UI Designer

Backpack Buddies


    • Project Brief

    Website Redesign for backpackbuddiesga.org, a non-profit organization, by implementing a modern design with intuitive navigation, clear content hierarchy, and r ensuring a seamless experience for visitors seeking information about the organization's mission, volunteer opportunities, and donation options.

    Problem

    Small, nonprofit organizations don’t usually have time or skill sets to develop clear websites to promote their mission and gain volunteers and donations.

    Goal

    - Create a redesigned website with a simple and user-friendly interface.
    - Incorporate clear call-to-actions (CTAs) throughout the website.
    - Strategically place buttons to improve navigation and user engagement.

    Impact

    The prototype testing results clearly demonstrated that the redesign has empowered users to easily locate donation options, volunteer opportunities, and the organization's mission statement

    We started by asking a few questions about our target audience for the Backpack Buddies website. We wanted to understand who our primary users were and what motivated them to visit our site.


    User Interviews

    To commence this project and gain a thorough understanding of the pain points, we conducted five semi-structured interviews via Zoom Meeting. The insights we gathered are as follows: Signing up for volunteering online can be confusing for users, who often struggle to find detailed information on events and volunteer opportunities beyond word of mouth. Despite the abundance of information on social media platforms such as Instagram, many users still face difficulty in accessing and signing up for volunteer programs online.


    Surveys

    From the 81 responses received for the survey, the following insights were gathered:

    • -75% of users reported spending time volunteering and found it enjoyable, but rarely used volunteering websites.
    • -98% of these users relied on word of mouth or social media to discover volunteering opportunities.
    • -66% of users reported difficulty in finding ways to offer financial aid or dedicate their time through the websites.

    Usability testing of existing website

    From the five users

    • -98% of users struggled with the word “give” for donations
    • -75% found having a second “Give” underneath the first was odd, didn’t initially look at “Who We Are” for “About Us”, noted that there should be a “Donate” button globally accessible throughout the site.



    Affinity Map

    Feature Prioritization

    With our important insights determined, we created a feature prioritization matrix to see what we could implement that was also a priority for the business and/or the user. 



    We were able to identify several usability issues and areas for improvement in the initial design. Based on the feedback and observations from the usability testing, we made necessary revisions and refinements to the mid-fi prototype, resulting in a more user-friendly and intuitive design. The revised prototype was then tested again, and we observed significant improvements in user engagement and task completion rates. Overall, the prototyping and testing phase allowed us to refine and validate the design, ensuring a better user experience for the Backpack Buddies project.

    Next Steps

    We want to put more accessibility features into the site. Features like:

    • -The ability to change the size of the text
    • -The ability to change the font to an easier one to read for dyslexia
    • -The ability to change the colors to higher contrast

    This was an amazing experience and everyone really came together to produce a product that we’re all proud of. Although collaborating presented its challenges, we tackled them head-on by communicating effectively and actively listening to each other, resulting in a cohesive end product.
